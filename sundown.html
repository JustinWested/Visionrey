Sundown

"...an intimate and touching experience"

Sundown is a short film about a young caretaker concealing a burdening secret from the elderly woman she cares for.

Why Is This Film Important?

  • It is believed that over 9 million Americans live with some form of Dementia today.
  • Billions are spent each year on rising healthcare costs relating to dementia. In addition to financial burdens, family and care partners looking after loved ones are often emotionally frustrated and physically stretched.
  • Over 11 million Americans provide unpaid care for people with dementia & Alzheimer’s disease.
  • In 2022, unpaid caregivers provided an estimated 18 BILLION hours of care valued at $339.5 billion dollars.
  • Today there are no cures or effective long term treatments

For more information and to donate to Alzheimer's research, please reach out to the Dementia Society of America & the Alzheimer's Association

The Team

Director

Tony Marquez

Tony is a Filipino American filmmaker based in Los Angeles. Originally from the Washington DC metro area, his love of storytelling grew from playing with cameras as a way of visual self-expression to understanding the power it has to connect and empathize with people who were far different than him. His passion took him to George Mason University, where he graduated with a Bachelor of Arts in Film and Video Studies, but his curiosity drew him to continue learning by enrolling in post-grad classes at the VA Film Office, Women in Film & Video DC, and Arlington Independent Media. He landed jobs in non-scripted television with Travel Channel and Discovery Networks before transitioning to a career in scripted formats.

Pulling from his experiences of traveling the country on unscripted productions, attending one of the most diverse Universities in the country, and relocating to a different coast, he aims to tell stories that captivate audiences of all backgrounds. Despite the long days and months working on sets, he’s constantly made time to create independent projects to further his pursuit of directing narrative films.

Tony has been fortunate to work on and learn from shows such as Lethal Weapon, Criminal Minds, and Young Sheldon. He was accepted into the Sundance Director Core Masterclass in 2020 and created two microbudget short films during the ongoing pandemic. In March 2022, he became a DGA member and works as a 2nd Assistant Director under the New York Qualifications List and Commercial Qualifications List.

He recently finished a new project titled “FilAm,” a deeply personal story that he hopes will shed light on his own Filipino American experience and is looking to add it into the festival circuit. His next project is titled “Sundown,” a story about a young caretaker watching over an elderly dementia patient. His previous work has found success at the Boston International Film Festival, Richmond International Film Festival, and Olympus Film Festival in Los Angeles.



Screenwriter

Kathleen Bath

Kathleen is a writer and director for the stage and screen with a strong background in theater. She graduated from George Mason University with a B.F.A. in Theater with concentrations in Performance (acting and directing) and Playwriting/Screenwriting and Dramaturgy. She's directed a variety of stage productions for various theatre companies in the D.C. area and beyond. She also wrote and directed the short film, The Death of Anne Brontë, which has screened at a number of film festivals online and across the United States.



Producer

Allison Marie Reyes

Allison is an award winning producer and actress. A true multi-hyphenate, she now produces some of the biggest shows and films in the industry. Her most notable projects include, DC’s Blue Beetle, Zack Snyders’ Rebel Moon, and Hype House - A Netflix Top Ten!

As an actress, Allison is known for her role of Kassandra in Shine Bright. She can also be seen in a few commercials for Realtor.com and most recently a national commercial for CVS!

Allison is the founder and CEO of VisionRey Entertainment - a production company that specializes in films and helping people in underrepresented communities ignite their careers in entertainment. It is Allison’s hope to be able to give back to the community that has helped her become who she is today.



Producer

Enrique Cruz Torres

Enrique is a Mexican American filmmaker. He holds a Bachelors's in Film and Digital Media (BFA) and a Master's of Business Administration (M.B.A) from the University of New Mexico. Born and raised in a Spanish-speaking household, Enrique discovered and quickly understood the power of film and visual media as a universal language.



Director of Photography

Bailey Softness

Bailey graduated from Wesleyan University in 2017 with a degree in English and Film Studies. After spending the summer of 2017 shooting the short documentary “Habesha” in Israel she moved to LA to pursue working in camera full time. After working as a Set PA in reality tv and working in camera on short films and non-union projects, she joined Local 600 in 2019 as a Digital Utility/Loader and has worked in the camera department on projects including The Curse of La Llorona, Annabelle Comes Home, Jumanji:The Next Level, Lethal Weapon, YOU, and Reservation Dogs. She is currently on her third season as the Loader/Additional 2nd AC on Young Sheldon.



Music Artist

Mackenzie Ryan

Mackenzie is a professionally trained vocalist, self-taught multi-instrumentalist and burgeoning producer, Mack utilizes her uniquely powerful voice to perform a wide variety of classic and contemporary musical selections in a way that is at once subtly nuanced and refreshingly vibrant. Part rock ‘n’ roll, a little country, some funk, a touch of soul and all heart, Mack’s music is as perfectly suited for bars and wineries as it is for the big stage.

Her acoustic solo set is filled with a diverse mix of sing-along songs from Van Morrison and Creedence Clearwater Revival to Smokey Robinson and The Lumineers. She is currently looking for a full-band setup.

She takes creative inspiration from Etta James, Frank Ocean and The Rolling Stones and credits Stevie Nicks and Led Zeppelin for her musical motivation. She is currently working on her first EP.

Mack, a native Virginian and George Mason graduate, plays public and private shows in and around Virginia, Maryland and Washington D.C.

She has opened for the southern-rock group Jason Morton and The Chesapeake Sons, country stars Nick Norman, Lewis Brice, Phil Vassar, Michael Ray and Montgomery Gentry.
